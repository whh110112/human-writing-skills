# Character Consistency Audit

## Aim

Detect character drift without freezing a character into one trait. Check whether
voice, goals, fears, knowledge, abilities, boundaries, status, and decisions remain
continuous or change through an earned on-page event.

## Character State Card

For each important character, extract:

- Current goal and immediate tactic
- Public persona and private pressure
- Stable voice markers: vocabulary, directness, humor, titles, taboo phrases
- Knowledge and mistaken beliefs
- Skills, limits, injuries, resources, rank, and obligations
- Loyalties, hostilities, attraction, shame, fear, and boundaries
- Recent event capable of changing any of the above

## Drift Types

- Voice swap: two characters become interchangeable or use the wrong social register.
- Goal abandonment: a character forgets what they were trying to obtain or prevent.
- Knowledge reset/leak: they forget a discovery or know something never revealed.
- Competence drift: skill, strength, authority, courage, or intelligence changes for convenience.
- Boundary drift: a refusal, loyalty, taboo, trauma, promise, or secret disappears.
- Emotion without bridge: attitude reverses without event, thought, evidence, or accumulating pressure.
- Rank mismatch: address forms and behavior ignore hierarchy, intimacy, or public posture.
- Author convenience: a character explains, forgives, confesses, attacks, or cooperates only to move the plot.

## Change Test

A contradiction may be valid development if the draft shows at least one change gate:

- new evidence
- accumulated cost or pressure
- explicit decision
- betrayal, loss, reward, threat, or changed leverage
- private action that conflicts with public posture
- deliberate lie, performance, manipulation, or loss of control

When no gate exists, flag the drift. When a gate exists but is too weak, recommend
strengthening the bridge rather than restoring the old behavior.
