# Scene Entry Audit

## Aim

Prevent scene and chapter openings from using a repeated cinematic setup bundle:
exact clock time, named location, weather or colored light, full appearance inventory,
ambient smell, and a summarized feeling before anyone has to do anything.

## Activation

Use inside the optional `texture` profile when an opening stacks several setup
channels or repeatedly begins from a fresh scenic slate. Do not activate merely
because one useful time, place, uniform, or weather detail appears.

## Pressure-First Map

For each opening, identify:

- inherited pressure: the cost, question, desire, threat, obligation, or unfinished act
- first usable anchor: one time, place, object, body state, or social cue needed now
- first choice or interference: what prevents routine continuation
- delayed details: information that can wait until an action makes it relevant

An opening may be quiet. It still needs a directional pressure, such as avoiding a
message, hiding an injury, arriving late, recognizing an object, or choosing not to
answer.

## Audit

Flag an opening only when several details arrive as a presentation package and most
remain inert. Also flag repeated chapter starts that discard prior residue and rebuild
time, weather, clothes, and mood from zero.

Keep exact clocks for schedules, alibis, countdowns, records, shifts, travel, or comic
timing. Keep uniforms and location names when hierarchy, access, movement, or evidence
uses them. The problem is simultaneous display without consequence.

## Repair

Keep the smallest orienting detail set. Enter through pressure or a concrete task,
then reveal appearance and atmosphere when perception or action earns them. Do not
replace the bundle with a louder hook or another decorative metaphor.
