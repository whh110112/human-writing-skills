# Embodied Emotion Skill

## Aim

Turn emotion into perception, body response, action, and contradiction. Avoid naming the feeling before the reader can sense it.

## Technique

- Identify two emotions that can coexist in the moment.
- Choose one visible action and one involuntary body signal.
- Distort one sense in a way that belongs to the emotion.
- Let the character or speaker misread themselves before understanding.
- Use objects as emotional witnesses: cups, sleeves, dust, receipts, door handles.

## Emotion Transform Examples

| Emotion | Less Useful | More Embodied |
| --- | --- | --- |
| anxiety | "She was anxious." | "The room kept making small noises. Her thumb found the edge of the receipt and worried it soft." |
| grief | "He felt sad." | "He rinsed the same bowl twice and still left rice at the bottom." |
| anger | "He was furious." | "He answered gently, which was how she knew he had stopped listening." |
| shame | "She was embarrassed." | "The back of her neck warmed before anyone said her name." |

## Avoid

- melodrama in every emotional beat
- stacking all five senses at once
- body reactions that do not fit the character or culture
- explaining the emotion immediately after showing it

## Revision Check

If the emotion label is removed, can the reader still feel what is happening?
