# Serial Reentry Skill

## Aim

Restore only the prior information a returning reader needs, through present action
and pressure instead of a chapter-opening recap dump.

## Activation Gate

Use only when prior chapters, a continuity ledger, or an explicit serialized-story
brief is supplied. If no prior material exists, do not infer past events and do not
activate this module.

## Reentry Card

Before drafting or auditing, select at most five live carryovers:

- immediate location, time, posture, injury, clothing, or held object
- unresolved promise, threat, question, debt, or misunderstanding
- relationship temperature and information permissions
- one world rule, resource, or consequence needed in this chapter
- one voice, object, phrase, or sensory residue that can recall the prior scene

Everything else stays in the ledger until the new chapter actually needs it.

## Techniques

- Reintroduce a fact when it changes a current choice.
- Let an object, altered nickname, injury, unfinished message, or avoided question
  carry the reminder.
- Place orientation near the first relevant action, not automatically in paragraph one.
- Assume regular readers remember broad premises; refresh exact local pressure.
- For a long gap or new volume, expand orientation gradually across several beats.

## Audit

Flag recap blocks that list relationships, powers, appearances, or past events before
they matter. Also flag missing carryovers that make the chapter appear to reset.

```text
- Prior material supplied:
- Live carryovers used now:
- Premature recap or repeated exposition:
- Missing residue or reset:
- Minimal insertion, delay, or cut:
```
