# Detail Disclosure Audit

## Aim

Prevent character introductions and scene openings from reading like database records.
Specific detail should arrive when perception, action, relationship, or plot earns it.

## Detail Classes

- Operational now: affects movement, choice, evidence, hierarchy, or risk.
- Naturally visible: the viewpoint would notice it in this moment.
- Relationship-bearing: changes attraction, distrust, recognition, or status.
- Delayed value: useful later, but stronger when revealed by action.
- Decorative inventory: precise but inert information.

## Audit

Flag clusters that introduce age, height, weight, occupation, marriage history,
appearance, clothing, and personality before the scene uses them. Also flag full-body
camera scans and exact biographical facts unsupported by narrator knowledge.

Exact details are valid when they are records, clues, professional observations,
rules, jokes, self-presentations, or later payoffs. The issue is timing and function,
not specificity itself.

## Repair

Keep the two details doing the most work. Delay, distribute, convert to action, or cut
the rest. Do not replace concrete information with generic adjectives.
