# Dialogue Voice Audit

## Aim

Keep characters distinguishable without turning each speaker into a catchphrase.
Audit how a person chooses words, answers pressure, changes register, and handles
silence across scenes and audiences.

## Activation Gate

Use only when the draft contains sustained dialogue, multiple speakers, or an
explicit request to distinguish character voices. Do not load for narration-only
passages, reports, or generic proofreading.

## Voice Fingerprint

For each recurring speaker, extract evidence for:

- sentence and turn length
- direct answer, evasion, counterquestion, joke, command, or silence
- vocabulary level, idiom, profession, dialect, and taboo wording
- titles, names, pronouns, politeness, and profanity
- interruption, repetition, self-correction, and unfinished syntax
- public register versus private register
- what the speaker notices, refuses to name, or habitually explains

Use tendencies, not rigid quotas. A frightened or performing character may depart
from the fingerprint when the scene supplies a reason.

## Contrast Test

1. Remove speaker labels from several representative lines.
2. Compare the lines against the fingerprints.
3. Flag interchangeable speakers, borrowed catchphrases, or unexplained register shifts.
4. Check whether the listener and audience justify the variation.
5. Repair the smallest unit: wording, response tactic, address form, or silence.

## Output

```text
Dialogue Voice Audit
- Speaker fingerprint and evidence:
- Lines that could belong to anyone:
- Unexplained voice or register drift:
- Intentional performance or pressure change:
- Minimal repair:
```

Do not invent dialect or verbal tics merely to make speakers different.
