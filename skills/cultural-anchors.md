# Cultural Anchors Skill

## Aim

Prevent vacuum prose by grounding writing in era, place, class, profession, community, and shared memory.

## Anchor Types

- material objects: phones, snacks, furniture, tickets, uniforms, tools
- institutional details: school rules, office habits, hospital queues, housing compounds
- social temperature: what people are afraid to say, proud of, tired of, or pretending not to know
- language layer: idioms, address terms, borrowed words, local phrasing
- public memory: indirect echoes of widely known events, songs, slogans, or trends

## Use Carefully

- Do not explain every cultural marker.
- Let the detail work through context.
- Prefer a small accurate object over a grand historical summary.
- Avoid stereotypes; anchor a specific person, not a whole group.
- If uncertain, mark the detail as needing fact-checking rather than inventing confidently.

## Coordinate Format

When possible, provide a cultural coordinate:

```text
Place: 1990s county town in southern China
Class/occupation: middle-school teacher family
Social pressure: stable work matters more than personal desire
Language: restrained, family nicknames, a few local turns
Objects: enamel mug, bus station loudspeaker, work-unit notice board
```

## Revision Check

Could this passage happen anywhere, in any decade, to anyone? If yes, add one precise anchor.
