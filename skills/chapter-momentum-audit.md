# Chapter Momentum Audit

## Aim

Check whether serialized chapters deliver movement rather than only atmosphere,
explanation, or an unrelated final shock.

## Activation

Use only for a multi-chapter draft, a chapter batch, or an explicit request to inspect
serial pacing and chapter payoff. It does not require prior chapters; use
`serial-reentry` separately when external continuity material is supplied.

## Chapter Contract

Map every chapter with five fields:

1. Entry pressure: what is already unstable or desired.
2. Irreversible turn: what becomes newly true.
3. Promised payoff: which title, premise, question, or setup receives progress.
4. Residue: which cost, object, secret, injury, debt, or relationship change survives.
5. Exit pressure: what specific next choice or consequence remains open.

A chapter need not contain a large twist. A refusal, permission, discovery, failed
attempt, shifted alliance, spent resource, or changed interpretation can be enough.

## Audit

- Flag chapters whose first section rebuilds weather, clothes, room, and mood while
  ignoring the previous chapter's residue.
- Flag dialogue that confirms an existing attraction or conflict without changing
  access, knowledge, leverage, risk, or commitment.
- Flag promised scenes delayed by recaps, biographies, world rules, or decorative
  description that the current choice does not use.
- Flag hooks that introduce an unrelated threat only because the chapter itself left
  no pressure.
- Distinguish deliberate rest chapters from accidental resets. A rest chapter should
  still alter recovery, trust, planning, resources, or expectation.

## Repair

Repair the earliest missing link. Add or sharpen one choice, consequence, failed
attempt, or carried residue. Do not solve weak momentum by stacking cliffhangers.
