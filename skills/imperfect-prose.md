# Imperfect Prose Skill

## Aim

Permit controlled roughness so the prose does not feel machine-polished. Human writing often has seams: repetition, self-correction, clipped fragments, and small hesitations.

## Friction Types

- Oral filler: "how to put it", "I mean", "maybe not quite"
- Search-for-word revision: "bright--no, white, the kind of white that hurts"
- Intentional repetition: repeat a name, pronoun, or phrase when pressure rises
- Fragment sentence: use a sentence without a full grammatical spine for impact
- Local idiom: use region, profession, generation, or family-specific wording when context supports it

## Genre Permissions

| Genre | Allowed Roughness |
| --- | --- |
| Fiction | fragments, repeated pronouns, interrupted dialogue, self-correction |
| Essay | limited hesitation, personal aside, uneven but intentional emphasis |
| Self-media | spoken rhythm, mild redundancy, colloquial turns |
| Web novel | repeated names/titles, clipped action beats, reader-facing pressure |
| News report | very little; clarity and attribution matter more |
| Academic paper | almost none; use cautious qualification instead |

## Avoid

- fake typos
- random grammar mistakes
- roughness that damages clarity
- slang that does not belong to the speaker, era, or audience

## Revision Check

Each imperfection must sound like a person thinking, speaking, or remembering under pressure. It must not look like carelessness.
