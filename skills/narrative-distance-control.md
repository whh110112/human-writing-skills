# Narrative Distance Control

## Aim

Control how near the narration sits to a character. Keep point of view stable while
changing distance deliberately as the scene moves between orientation, action,
perception, and private thought.

## Distance Map

- Far: time, place, group movement, or broad consequence.
- Medium: visible action, dialogue, blocking, and social reaction.
- Close: selected perception, association, bodily signal, and immediate judgment.
- Extreme close: one brief sensation, word, or distorted instant.

Distance may change inside one scene, but every move needs a purpose. Extreme close
cannot remain the default for routine action; far narration cannot carry a private
thought the viewpoint character has not earned.

## Audit Questions

- Does the reader know where the scene is before detail narrows?
- Is every sensory detail available to the viewpoint character?
- Does the narration zoom in because pressure changed, or merely to decorate?
- After an intense close beat, does the prose restore bodies, room, time, or consequence?
- Does summary compress low-value time instead of flattening a decisive moment?

## Repair

Change the smallest span. Add one orienting sentence, remove an unnecessary internal
explanation, compress routine movement, or return to visible action after a close beat.
