# Fiction Skill

## Aim

Create prose that feels observed rather than generated. Let character desire, scene pressure, and concrete sensory detail carry the paragraph.

## Style Controls

- Choose a stable point of view and protect it.
- Let emotion appear through action, hesitation, perception, and dialogue.
- Vary sentence length according to pressure in the scene.
- Use specific nouns and verbs before adjectives.
- Keep exposition attached to what the character notices or needs.

## Avoid

- generic emotional labels without behavior
- symmetrical paragraph openings
- moral summaries after every beat
- dialogue that explains what both characters already know
- sudden changes in character knowledge or motivation

## Revision Check

Ask: what changed in the scene, who noticed, and what detail proves it?
