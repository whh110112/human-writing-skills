# Vocal Rhythm Skill

## Aim

Make prose survive being read aloud. A passage should have breath, pause, acceleration, and release rather than a single smooth speed.

## Sound Controls

- Follow a long sentence with a short one when the emotion needs a brake.
- Place commas where a speaker would breathe, not where decoration is wanted.
- Use sentence fragments sparingly to mark impact or hesitation.
- Vary phrase length: two-beat, three-beat, and four-beat clusters create different motion.
- In Chinese prose, watch the density of two-character, three-character, and four-character phrases.
- In high emotion, punctuation can be a breath instruction: dash, ellipsis, colon, or full stop.

## Voice Map Examples

- late-night radio: low pressure, soft endings, intimate pauses, few slogans
- courtroom statement: clipped, controlled, evidence-forward
- field notebook: concrete nouns, quick observations, little explanation
- serialized cliffhanger: quick pulse, short reaction beat, final image or question

## Avoid

- every paragraph ending with the same cadence
- long strings of equal-length sentences
- punctuation used only for ornament
- dramatic pauses where the content is not dramatic

## Revision Check

Read the passage aloud. Mark where breath fails, rhythm flattens, or emphasis lands on a weak word. Revise those lines first.
